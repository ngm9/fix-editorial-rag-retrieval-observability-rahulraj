# Digital Advertising Revenue Trends in Media: A 2024 Analysis

Digital advertising revenue across media companies rebounded sharply in the first half of 2024 following a contraction in 2022 and a slow recovery in 2023. Total digital ad spend in the United States reached $225 billion for the full year, a 12% increase year-over-year, according to the Interactive Advertising Bureau.

Connected TV (CTV) advertising emerged as the fastest-growing segment, expanding 28% year-over-year to reach $30 billion. Streaming platforms that launched ad-supported tiers in 2022 and 2023 began to see material revenue contributions, with Netflix reporting $1.7 billion in advertising revenue for 2024, its first full year of disclosed ad financials.

Programmatic advertising automation accounted for 72% of all digital display ad transactions by volume. Demand-side platforms processed an average of 14 trillion bid requests per day globally, though actual fill rates for premium inventory remained under 40% due to floor pricing policies maintained by major publishers.

First-party data strategies became critical for publishers following the delayed but eventual deprecation of third-party cookies in Chrome. Publishers that had invested in authenticated identity solutions — such as email-based login walls — reported CPMs 60% higher than those relying on contextual targeting alone.

Podcast advertising grew to $2.4 billion in the United States, driven primarily by host-read endorsements and a shift toward dynamic ad insertion technology that allowed advertisers to target episodes based on listener demographics rather than show topics alone.

Social media platforms faced headwinds from brand-safety concerns, with a consortium of 200 advertisers issuing guidelines restricting spend on platforms where AI-generated content could not be distinguished from human-authored posts. This impacted revenue at platforms with lower moderation investment.

Local news publishers reported that despite national ad market recovery, local digital advertising remained depressed, with average CPMs for local news inventory 45% lower than national equivalents. Membership and reader-revenue models have partially offset this gap, with the Texas Tribune and The Atlantic cited as successful hybrid models.

Affiliate commerce content — articles that earn commissions on product purchases — grew to represent 18% of total revenue for mid-tier digital publishers, up from 9% in 2020. This shift raised editorial independence questions that several news organizations addressed through published disclosure policies.
